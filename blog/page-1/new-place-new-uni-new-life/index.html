New Place, New Uni, New Life

New Place, New Uni, New Life

Linton University Collge, I got a free degree offer from this school. Without thinking much, I enrolled and flied all the way from Sabah to Negeri Sembilan. I never knew Mantin or Nilai until I reached and lived in my uni hostel for a week.

The hostel I lived in is cheap, but still there are things that I do not like about it. For instance, the shared washroom, the flush and tap were destroyed by the previous tenant. This kind of vandalism, I never thought would happen among university students. Moral of the story: University gives you knowledge but not your personality.

Cycling around Mantin Town

From what I see and learn, the place I studied, the area is known as Mantin, it is a small town. And majority of the residents live here, from what I met are Indian and Chinese. Majority is still Malay.

I remember first day I arrived here at Linton. After unpacking and dropping my luggages at hostel, the first thing I do is to find a tranportation. I walked from school to the town area. I treated it as a day trip. I walked, stopped, ate and continued. I found quite many motorcycle shops. However without a license, I came to a decision to buy a bicycle.

Riding on the bike, facing the breeze, I never felt so refresh. Mantin is a place where you can find peace and harmony. People here are nice and not racist. I cycled around the city until I reached a signboard stated 'See you again. Selamat Jalan.'

That day was Sunday, and I was lucky to visit the Pasar Malam. According to the locals, the Pasar Malam only opens on Sunday. While my stomach is growling, I ordered a Bakso and have a bite. The cost was only RM 4.50, which for me, is considered quite cheap and affordable.

My journey ended with a stall at the roadside. I stopped my bike, and have a durian feast, the kampung durian. I ate one. It tasted good. Riding back to hostel, seeing people here living a harmony and peaceful lifestyle, I grinned, pondering my life in a new place, new uni. 2020, please treat me well.
